The Heat Equation

Deriving $\frac{\partial u}{\partial t} = \alpha\,\nabla^2 u$ from first principles.

✏️ Narrative content coming soon — fill in the derivation and physical intuition here.

Setup: a 1D rod

Consider a thin, insulated rod of length $L$. Let $u(x,t)$ be the temperature at position $x$ and time $t$. We want to know how $u$ evolves.

Energy balance on a small slice

The result is: $$\frac{\partial u}{\partial t} = \alpha\,\frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial x^2}, \qquad \alpha = \frac{k}{\rho\, c_p}$$

Boundary & initial conditions

Fourier's solution

Using the separation of variables Fourier discovered: $$u(x,t) = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} B_n \sin\!\left(\frac{n\pi x}{L}\right) e^{-\alpha(n\pi/L)^2 t}$$
