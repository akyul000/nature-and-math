The wave equation

$$\frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial t^2} = c^2\,\frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial x^2}$$

D'Alembert's solution

The general solution is a superposition of left- and right-traveling waves: $$u(x,t) = f(x - ct) + g(x + ct)$$

Finite propagation speed

Reflections and boundary conditions

Physical examples

  • Vibrating string: $u_{tt} = c^2\,u_{xx}$, $c = \sqrt{T/\mu}$
  • Acoustics: pressure waves in air, $c \approx 343$ m/s
  • Electromagnetic waves: Maxwell's equations reduce to the wave equation
  • Seismic waves: P-waves and S-waves through the Earth
